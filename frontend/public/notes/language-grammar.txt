Language Notes: Grammar
========================

PARTS OF SPEECH
- Noun: name of person, place, thing, idea (types: proper, common, collective, abstract, material)
- Pronoun: replaces noun (personal, reflexive, relative, interrogative, demonstrative)
- Verb: action or state (transitive vs intransitive; main vs auxiliary)
- Adjective: modifies noun (qualitative, quantitative, demonstrative, interrogative)
- Adverb: modifies verb/adjective/adverb (manner, time, place, degree)
- Preposition: shows relationship (in, on, at, by, with, of, for, since, until)
- Conjunction: joins clauses (coordinating: FANBOYS; subordinating: because, although)
- Interjection: expresses emotion (Oh! Wow! Alas!)

TENSES (12 Tenses)
- Simple: Present/Past/Future
- Continuous: Present/Past/Future + (to be + V-ing)
- Perfect: Present/Past/Future + (to have + V3)
- Perfect Continuous: Present/Past/Future + (to have been + V-ing)

SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT
- Singular subject → singular verb; Plural subject → plural verb
- Collective nouns (team, class): usually singular
- "Either/Neither" with two subjects: verb agrees with nearer subject
- "Along with / as well as / together with": verb agrees with first subject

SENTENCE TRANSFORMATION
- Active to Passive: Object + (to be + V3) + by + Subject
- Direct to Indirect speech: change tense, pronouns, time expressions
- Affirmative to Negative/Interrogative transformations
- Simple to Complex/Compound: using subordinating/coordinating conjunctions

EXAM TIPS
- Active-passive and direct-indirect are highest scoring areas
- Practice tense identification in paragraph correction
- Subject-verb agreement errors appear in error detection questions
