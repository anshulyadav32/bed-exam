Language Notes: Comprehension
==============================

READING COMPREHENSION STRATEGY
- Step 1: Skim the passage for main idea (30 seconds)
- Step 2: Read questions before reading the passage fully
- Step 3: Read passage carefully, underlining key information
- Step 4: Answer factual questions first, then inferential
- Step 5: Return to passage to verify each answer

TYPES OF QUESTIONS
- Direct/Factual: answer explicitly stated in passage
- Inferential: answer implied, not directly stated
- Vocabulary in context: meaning of a word/phrase as used in passage
- Title/Theme: identify the best title or central idea
- Author's tone: objective, critical, appreciative, ironic, humorous

ERROR DETECTION
- Check subject-verb agreement first
- Look for tense consistency throughout sentence
- Preposition errors: "interested in" not "interested on"
- Article errors: a/an/the usage rules
- Pronoun-antecedent agreement

SENTENCE IMPROVEMENT
- Identify the grammatically incorrect or awkward part
- Check for: redundancy, incorrect word form, wrong preposition
- "No improvement required" is often a valid option — don't change unnecessarily

CLOZE TEST
- Read the full passage first to understand context
- Fill blanks based on grammatical and semantic fit
- Check: part of speech required, collocations, tone of passage
- Eliminate options that change the meaning

PARA JUMBLES
- Find the opening sentence (no pronoun reference, introduces topic)
- Find the closing sentence (conclusion, summary)
- Link sentences by: pronouns, connectives, repeated words

EXAM TIPS
- Practice one comprehension passage daily
- For cloze tests: grammar + meaning must both fit
- Error detection: all four parts of a sentence may be correct
