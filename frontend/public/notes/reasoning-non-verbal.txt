Reasoning Notes: Non-Verbal Reasoning
=======================================

PATTERN RECOGNITION
- Identify the rule (rotation, reflection, addition, removal of elements)
- Look at rows AND columns in a matrix
- Changes can be: size, shading, number of sides, orientation

MIRROR IMAGE
- Vertical mirror: image is flipped horizontally (left ↔ right)
- Horizontal mirror: image is flipped vertically (top ↔ bottom)
- For clocks: add or subtract from 12 (mirror time = 11:60 – original time)

WATER IMAGE
- Image is flipped vertically (top ↔ bottom)
- Left-right stays the same

PAPER FOLDING & CUTTING
- Visualise the fold in reverse when unfolding
- Symmetric holes: a fold + cut gives holes on both halves
- Count total holes after full unfolding

FIGURE COMPLETION / EMBEDDED FIGURES
- Complete the figure using the given options
- Look for symmetry and proportion
- In embedded figures, rotate the small figure mentally

COUNTING FIGURES
- Triangles in a big triangle: use formula n(n+2)(2n+1)/8
- Squares in a grid: 1×1 + 2×2 + 3×3... (sum of squares formula)
- Rectangles: nC₂ × nC₂ for an n×n grid

SERIES OF FIGURES
- Track changes from one figure to the next
- Changes: rotation (45°, 90°, 180°), addition/removal of elements, shading swap

EXAM TIPS
- Practice mirror images of alphabets: A, H, I, M, O, T, U, V, W, X, Y are symmetric
- Paper folding: always unfold mentally step by step
- 30% of non-verbal questions involve rotation — master 90° and 180° turns
