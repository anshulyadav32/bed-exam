GK Notes: Indian Polity
=======================

CONSTITUTION OF INDIA
- Adopted: 26 November 1949; Enforced: 26 January 1950
- Longest written constitution in the world
- Borrowed features: UK (parliamentary system), USA (fundamental rights), Ireland (DPSP), Canada (federal structure)
- Preamble: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic

FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS (Part III, Articles 12-35)
- Right to Equality (14-18)
- Right to Freedom (19-22)
- Right against Exploitation (23-24)
- Right to Freedom of Religion (25-28)
- Cultural & Educational Rights (29-30)
- Right to Constitutional Remedies (32) — "Heart and Soul" of the Constitution

FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES (Article 51-A, 11 duties added by 42nd Amendment)

DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES OF STATE POLICY (Part IV)
- Non-justiciable but fundamental to governance

GOVERNMENT STRUCTURE
- Executive: President → Prime Minister → Council of Ministers
- Legislature: Parliament = Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha
- Judiciary: Supreme Court → High Courts → District Courts

PANCHAYATI RAJ
- 73rd Amendment (1992): constitutional status to local self-governance
- Three-tier system: Gram Panchayat, Panchayat Samiti, Zila Parishad

EXAM TIPS
- Article numbers of FRs are often asked
- Know emergency provisions (352, 356, 360)
- Remember writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition, Quo Warranto
