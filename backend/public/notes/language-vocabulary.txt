Language Notes: Vocabulary
===========================

SYNONYMS & ANTONYMS
- Learn in pairs: Benevolent (kind) ↔ Malevolent (evil)
- Common synonym pairs tested: Arduous=Difficult, Candid=Frank, Eloquent=Articulate
- Common antonym pairs: Abundant ↔ Scarce, Conceal ↔ Reveal, Frugal ↔ Extravagant
- Context-based synonyms: same word can have different synonyms in different contexts

ONE WORD SUBSTITUTION
- Omnivore: one who eats both plants and animals
- Bibliophile: lover of books
- Philatelist: stamp collector
- Misanthrope: one who dislikes all people
- Indefatigable: one who never tires
- Ambivert: combination of introvert and extrovert
- Posthumous: occurring after death
- Verbatim: word for word

IDIOMS AND PHRASES
- Beat around the bush: avoid the main topic
- Bite the bullet: endure a painful situation
- Hit the nail on the head: describe exactly what is happening
- Let the cat out of the bag: reveal a secret accidentally
- Once in a blue moon: rarely
- Burn the midnight oil: work late into the night
- Break the ice: initiate conversation in an awkward situation
- Cry over spilt milk: complain about past misfortunes

WORD USAGE & CONFUSABLES
- Affect (verb) vs Effect (noun)
- Principle (rule) vs Principal (head of institution/main)
- Stationary (not moving) vs Stationery (writing materials)
- Complement (completes) vs Compliment (praise)
- Accept (agree) vs Except (excluding)

EXAM TIPS
- Make flashcards for one-word substitutions (aim: 100+ words)
- Group idioms by theme (body parts, animals, weather)
- For confusables: make a mnemonic or example sentence
