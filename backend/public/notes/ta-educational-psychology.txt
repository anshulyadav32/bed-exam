Teaching Aptitude Notes: Educational Psychology
================================================

CHILD DEVELOPMENT
- Jean Piaget's Stages:
  * Sensorimotor (0-2 yrs): object permanence
  * Pre-operational (2-7 yrs): egocentric, symbolic play
  * Concrete operational (7-11 yrs): conservation, logical thinking
  * Formal operational (11+ yrs): abstract reasoning
- Vygotsky: Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD), scaffolding
- Erikson's 8 stages of psychosocial development

MOTIVATION
- Intrinsic motivation: driven by internal interest/enjoyment
- Extrinsic motivation: driven by external rewards/punishments
- Maslow's Hierarchy: Physiological → Safety → Love → Esteem → Self-Actualization
- Achievement Motivation: McClelland's theory (need for achievement)

LEARNING THEORIES
- Classical Conditioning (Pavlov): stimulus-response
- Operant Conditioning (Skinner): reinforcement (positive/negative), punishment
- Social Learning (Bandura): observational learning, self-efficacy
- Gestalt Theory: whole > sum of parts, insight learning
- Cognitive theories: schema, assimilation, accommodation

INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES
- Intelligence: Spearman's 'g' factor, Gardner's Multiple Intelligences (8 types)
- Learning styles: visual, auditory, kinesthetic (VAK model)
- Special needs: gifted, slow learners, learning disabilities (dyslexia, dyscalculia)
- Inclusive education principles

EXAM TIPS
- Piaget's stages with age groups are very frequently tested
- Know reinforcement types clearly
- Gardner's 8 intelligences: Linguistic, Logical-Mathematical, Spatial, Musical, Bodily-Kinesthetic, Interpersonal, Intrapersonal, Naturalist
