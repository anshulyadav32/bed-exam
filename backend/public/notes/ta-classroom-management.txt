Teaching Aptitude Notes: Classroom Management
==============================================

TIME MANAGEMENT
- Use timetable and lesson plan effectively
- Distribute time: 10% introduction, 70% development, 20% recapitulation
- Avoid wasting time on discipline issues by proactive management
- Bell-to-bell instruction: maximise learning time

INCLUSIVE PRACTICES
- Inclusive education: all children in mainstream classrooms regardless of ability
- Salamanca Statement (1994): UNESCO framework for inclusive education
- RTE Act 2009: free and compulsory education for 6-14 year olds
- Differentiated instruction: vary content, process, product based on learner readiness
- Universal Design for Learning (UDL): multiple means of representation, action, engagement

STUDENT BEHAVIOR
- Preventive discipline: clear rules, positive classroom climate
- Corrective discipline: immediate, consistent, fair response
- Positive reinforcement: praise, rewards for desired behavior
- Assertive discipline (Canter): clear expectations, consistent consequences
- Behavior modification: ABC (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence) model

ASSESSMENT TECHNIQUES
- Formative assessment: ongoing (quizzes, observations, exit tickets)
- Summative assessment: end of unit (tests, exams, projects)
- Continuous & Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE): assesses both scholastic and co-scholastic areas
- Portfolio assessment: collection of student work over time
- Peer and self-assessment: develops metacognitive skills

EXAM TIPS
- RTE Act provisions are commonly tested
- CCE pattern and its components are important
- Know difference between formative and summative assessment with examples
